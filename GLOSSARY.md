# Investment Research App - Glossary

This glossary explains financial and technical terms used throughout the application documentation. If you encounter a term you don't understand, check here first.

## üìä Technical Analysis Terms

### RSI (Relative Strength Index)
**What it is:** A technical indicator that measures whether a stock is overbought (too expensive) or oversold (too cheap).  
**How it works:** RSI ranges from 0 to 100. Values above 70 typically indicate overbought conditions, while values below 30 indicate oversold conditions.  
**Why it matters:** Helps traders identify potential entry and exit points.

### MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
**What it is:** A trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages of a stock's price.  
**How it works:** When the MACD line crosses above the signal line, it's typically a bullish (buy) signal. When it crosses below, it's bearish (sell).  
**Why it matters:** Helps identify trend changes and momentum shifts.

### Moving Averages (SMA, EMA)
**What it is:** A calculation that smooths out price data by creating a constantly updated average price.  
**SMA (Simple Moving Average):** Average of prices over a set period (e.g., 50-day average).  
**EMA (Exponential Moving Average):** Gives more weight to recent prices.  
**Why it matters:** Helps identify trends and support/resistance levels.

### Bollinger Bands
**What it is:** A volatility indicator that consists of a middle band (moving average) and two outer bands.  
**How it works:** When prices touch the upper band, the stock may be overbought. When they touch the lower band, it may be oversold.  
**Why it matters:** Helps identify volatility and potential price reversals.

### Support and Resistance Levels
**What it is:** Price levels where a stock tends to stop falling (support) or stop rising (resistance).  
**How it works:** Based on historical price action - these are psychological price points where buyers or sellers tend to act.  
**Why it matters:** Helps predict where prices might bounce or reverse.

## üí∞ Fundamental Analysis Terms

### P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)
**What it is:** The ratio of a company's share price to its earnings per share.  
**How it works:** A P/E of 20 means investors are willing to pay $20 for every $1 of earnings.  
**Why it matters:** Helps determine if a stock is overvalued or undervalued compared to its earnings.

### P/B Ratio (Price-to-Book)
**What it is:** The ratio of a company's market value to its book value (assets minus liabilities).  
**How it works:** A P/B below 1 suggests the stock may be undervalued.  
**Why it matters:** Useful for value investors looking for bargains.

### PEG Ratio (Price/Earnings to Growth)
**What it is:** P/E ratio divided by the earnings growth rate.  
**How it works:** A PEG below 1 may indicate an undervalued stock.  
**Why it matters:** Accounts for growth, making it more useful than P/E alone.

### ROE (Return on Equity)
**What it is:** A measure of how efficiently a company uses shareholders' money to generate profits.  
**How it works:** Calculated as net income divided by shareholder equity. Higher is generally better.  
**Why it matters:** Indicates management effectiveness and profitability.

### DCF (Discounted Cash Flow)
**What it is:** A valuation method that estimates a company's value based on its future cash flows.  
**How it works:** Projects future cash flows and discounts them to present value.  
**Why it matters:** Helps determine the intrinsic (true) value of a stock.

### EV/EBITDA
**What it is:** Enterprise Value divided by Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization.  
**How it works:** A valuation metric that accounts for debt and operating expenses.  
**Why it matters:** Useful for comparing companies with different capital structures.

## üìà Portfolio & Risk Terms

### Beta
**What it is:** A measure of a stock's volatility compared to the overall market.  
**How it works:** Beta of 1.0 means the stock moves with the market. Beta above 1.0 is more volatile, below 1.0 is less volatile.  
**Why it matters:** Helps assess portfolio risk and diversification.

### Sharpe Ratio
**What it is:** A measure of risk-adjusted return - how much return you get per unit of risk.  
**How it works:** Higher Sharpe ratio = better risk-adjusted performance.  
**Why it matters:** Helps compare investment strategies on a risk-adjusted basis.

### VaR (Value at Risk)
**What it is:** A statistical measure of potential portfolio losses over a specific time period.  
**How it works:** "95% VaR of $1,000" means there's a 5% chance of losing more than $1,000.  
**Why it matters:** Helps quantify and understand portfolio risk.

### Correlation
**What it is:** A measure of how two investments move in relation to each other.  
**How it works:** Correlation ranges from -1 (move opposite) to +1 (move together). 0 means no relationship.  
**Why it matters:** Helps build diversified portfolios - low correlation reduces risk.

### Volatility
**What it is:** A measure of how much a stock's price fluctuates.  
**How it works:** Higher volatility = larger price swings (both up and down).  
**Why it matters:** Indicates risk level - higher volatility = higher risk.

### Drawdown
**What it is:** The peak-to-trough decline in portfolio value during a specific period.  
**How it works:** If your portfolio goes from $10,000 to $8,000, that's a 20% drawdown.  
**Why it matters:** Shows the worst-case scenario and helps assess strategy risk.

## üéØ Options Trading Terms

### Greeks (Delta, Gamma, Theta, Vega)
**What it is:** Measures of how options prices change based on various factors.  
**Delta:** How much the option price changes when the stock price changes.  
**Gamma:** How much Delta changes when the stock price changes.  
**Theta:** How much the option price decreases as time passes (time decay).  
**Vega:** How much the option price changes with volatility changes.  
**Why it matters:** Essential for understanding and managing options risk.

### Implied Volatility
**What it is:** The market's expectation of how volatile a stock will be, based on options prices.  
**How it works:** Higher implied volatility = higher options prices.  
**Why it matters:** Helps determine if options are expensive or cheap.

### Options Chain
**What it is:** A table showing all available options contracts for a stock (different strike prices and expiration dates).  
**How it works:** Displays call and put options with their prices (premiums) and Greeks.  
**Why it matters:** Essential for options traders to find the right contract.

## üíµ Investment Terms

### ROI (Return on Investment)
**What it is:** The percentage gain or loss on an investment.  
**How it works:** (Current Value - Cost) / Cost √ó 100.  
**Why it matters:** Simple measure of investment performance.

### CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)
**What it is:** The average annual growth rate of an investment over a period.  
**How it works:** Accounts for compounding - shows smoothed annual return.  
**Why it matters:** Better than simple average for long-term investments.

### Dividend Yield
**What it is:** Annual dividend payment divided by stock price, expressed as a percentage.  
**How it works:** A $2 annual dividend on a $100 stock = 2% yield.  
**Why it matters:** Shows income return from dividend-paying stocks.

### DRIP (Dividend Reinvestment Plan)
**What it is:** Automatically reinvesting dividends to buy more shares.  
**How it works:** Instead of receiving cash, dividends buy fractional shares.  
**Why it matters:** Compound growth - dividends buy more shares, which pay more dividends.

### Cost Basis
**What it is:** The original price you paid for an investment, including fees.  
**How it works:** Used to calculate capital gains/losses for taxes.  
**Why it matters:** Essential for tax reporting and profit/loss calculations.

### Realized vs Unrealized Gains
**Realized Gains:** Profits from investments you've actually sold.  
**Unrealized Gains:** Paper profits from investments you still own.  
**Why it matters:** Only realized gains are taxable. Unrealized gains can disappear if prices fall.

## üîî App-Specific Terms

### Paper Trading
**What it is:** Simulated trading with virtual money - no real money at risk.  
**How it works:** You make trades using real market data, but with fake money.  
**Why it matters:** Practice trading strategies without financial risk.

### Backtesting
**What it is:** Testing an investment strategy against historical market data.  
**How it works:** Apply your strategy rules to past data to see how it would have performed.  
**Why it matters:** See if a strategy would have worked before risking real money.

### Watchlist
**What it is:** A list of stocks you're monitoring but haven't bought yet.  
**How it works:** Add stocks to track their prices and news without owning them.  
**Why it matters:** Helps you research and decide before investing.

### Portfolio
**What it is:** Your collection of investments (stocks, ETFs, etc.).  
**How it works:** Track all your holdings, their values, and performance in one place.  
**Why it matters:** See your overall investment picture and diversification.

### Asset Allocation
**What it is:** How your portfolio is divided among different asset types (stocks, bonds, cash, etc.).  
**How it works:** Expressed as percentages - e.g., 60% stocks, 30% bonds, 10% cash.  
**Why it matters:** Key to diversification and risk management.

## üì± Technical Terms

### Real-time Data
**What it is:** Market data that updates immediately as prices change.  
**Note:** "Real-time" in free apps is often delayed by 15-20 minutes. True real-time requires paid data feeds.  
**Why it matters:** More timely data = better trading decisions.

### API (Application Programming Interface)
**What it is:** A way for our app to get data from external services (like stock prices).  
**How it works:** Our app requests data from a data provider's API, which sends back the information.  
**Why it matters:** Allows us to access market data we don't generate ourselves.

### LocalStorage
**What it is:** Browser storage that saves data on your device.  
**How it works:** Your portfolios, watchlists, and preferences are saved in your browser.  
**Why it matters:** Your data persists between sessions, but only on that device/browser.
