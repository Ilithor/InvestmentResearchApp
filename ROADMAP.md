# Investment Research App - Product Roadmap

## üìñ Overview

The Investment Research App is a comprehensive web application designed to help investors of all types research, analyze, and track their investments. Whether you're planning for retirement, actively trading, or just starting your investment journey, this app provides the tools and insights you need to make informed decisions.

### What This App Does
- **Research investments** - Search and analyze stocks, ETFs, and other assets
- **Track portfolios** - Monitor your investments and performance
- **Make informed decisions** - Access data, charts, and analysis tools
- **Learn and improve** - Educational resources and investment journal

### What This App Does NOT Do
- ‚ùå **Not a broker** - You cannot buy or sell stocks through this app
- ‚ùå **Not financial advice** - We provide tools and data, not investment recommendations
- ‚ùå **Not a replacement for professional advice** - Consult a financial advisor for personalized guidance
- ‚ùå **Not guaranteed accurate** - While we strive for accuracy, always verify critical data

### Important Notes
- **Data Sources**: Market data comes from third-party APIs (see Technology section). Free tiers may have delays (15-20 minutes) or rate limits.
- **Data Storage**: Your portfolios and preferences are stored locally in your browser. They won't sync across devices unless you export/import them.
- **Browser Compatibility**: Works best in modern browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge). Some features may not work in older browsers.
- **Mobile Experience**: The app is responsive and works on mobile devices, but some features are optimized for desktop use.

## ‚≠ê Standout Features (Coming Soon)

Beyond the standard features, we're building unique capabilities that set this app apart:

### üéØ Advanced Risk Management
**What it does:** Comprehensive risk analysis beyond basic metrics

**You get:**
- **Stress testing** - See how your portfolio would have performed during historical crashes (2008 financial crisis, COVID-19 crash, etc.)
- **"What if" scenarios** - Model different market conditions (e.g., "What if tech stocks drop 30%?")
- **Correlation heatmaps** - Visualize which stocks move together (helps identify over-concentration)
- **Value at Risk (VaR)** - Calculate potential losses at different confidence levels (e.g., "95% chance of losing no more than $X")
- **Tail risk analysis** - Identify exposure to extreme market events

**Example Use Case:**
You have a portfolio of tech stocks. The stress test shows that if tech crashes like it did in 2000, you'd lose 40% of your portfolio value. This helps you decide if you need more diversification.

**Why it matters:** Most apps don't offer this level of risk analysis. Understanding your portfolio's risk is crucial for long-term success.

---

### üß™ Strategy Builder & Backtesting
**What it does:** Create and test investment strategies before risking real money

**You get:**
- **Visual strategy builder** - Drag-and-drop interface to create trading rules (e.g., "Buy when RSI < 30 AND volume > average")
- **Historical backtesting** - Test your strategy against real historical market data to see how it would have performed
- **Paper trading mode** - Practice your strategy with virtual money in real-time (no real money at risk)
- **Performance metrics** - See Sharpe ratio (risk-adjusted return), max drawdown (worst loss), win rate, and more
- **Strategy comparison** - Test multiple strategies side-by-side to see which performs best

**Example Use Case:**
You create a strategy: "Buy when a stock's 50-day moving average crosses above its 200-day average." You backtest it on the last 5 years of data and find it would have returned 15% annually. You then paper trade it for a month before risking real money.

**Why it matters:** Test your ideas safely. Very few free apps offer robust backtesting capabilities.

---

### üîî Smart Alerts & Notifications
**What it does:** Intelligent, context-aware alerts that reduce noise

**You get:**
- **Pattern recognition alerts** - Get notified when technical patterns form (e.g., "AAPL: RSI dropped below 30 with 2x average volume - potential oversold condition")
- **Earnings calendar reminders** - Alerts before companies in your portfolio report earnings
- **News impact alerts** - Notifications when significant news affects stocks you own or watch
- **Portfolio rebalancing suggestions** - Alerts when your portfolio allocation drifts from your target (e.g., "Tech allocation is now 45%, target is 30%")
- **Custom alert logic** - Build your own alert conditions (e.g., "Alert me if any stock in my portfolio drops more than 5% in a day")

**Example Use Case:**
You set an alert: "Notify me if Apple stock drops below $150." When it happens, you get a notification (email or browser notification) so you can decide whether to buy.

**How it works:** Alerts are checked periodically (not instant). Free data sources may have delays. For real-time alerts, you'd need a paid data subscription.

**Why it matters:** Stay informed without being overwhelmed. Get alerts that actually matter to your investments.

---

### üìù Investment Journal
**What it does:** Learn from your investment decisions

**You get:**
- **Trade journal** - Record why you bought or sold each stock (e.g., "Bought AAPL because of strong iPhone sales and new product launches")
- **Decision tracking** - Tag trades with reasons (e.g., "technical analysis", "dividend play", "value opportunity")
- **Performance attribution** - See which types of decisions led to gains vs. losses (e.g., "My value picks returned 20%, but my momentum trades lost 5%")
- **Reflection prompts** - Periodic questions to review decisions (e.g., "Did your original investment thesis play out?")
- **Pattern identification** - Discover biases in your trading (e.g., "I tend to sell winners too early and hold losers too long")

**Example Use Case:**
You buy a stock because you think it's undervalued. You record this in your journal. Six months later, the stock drops 20%. You review your journal entry and realize you didn't consider the company's high debt. This helps you avoid similar mistakes.

**Why it matters:** Most investors don't learn from mistakes. This helps you improve over time.

5. **Try Advanced Features** - As you grow, explore backtesting and risk analysis

- You can update your investor type and preferences anytime in settings
- This will adjust which features are shown/hidden
- Your existing data (portfolios, watchlists) won't be affected
